## A: Accountant

### What is an Accountant?
An accountant is a financial professional responsible for managing, recording, and analyzing financial information for individuals, businesses, and organizations. They ensure financial accuracy, compliance with regulations, and help organizations make informed financial decisions.

### What Do They Do in a Company?
- **Financial Record Keeping:** Maintain accurate records of financial transactions
- **Bookkeeping:** Record income and expenses in accounting systems
- **Tax Preparation:** Prepare and file tax returns for compliance
- **Financial Reporting:** Generate financial statements (balance sheets, income statements, cash flow)
- **Auditing:** Review financial records for accuracy and regulatory compliance
- **Cost Analysis:** Analyze expenditures and identify cost-saving opportunities
- **Payroll Management:** Process employee salaries and deductions
- **Budget Planning:** Help develop company budgets and financial forecasts
- **Accounts Receivable/Payable:** Manage invoicing and payment processes

### Skills Required
- **Technical Skills:**
  - Proficiency in accounting software (SAGE, QuickBooks, Xero)
  - Excel and financial modeling
  - Understanding of IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards)
  - Tax knowledge and compliance understanding
  - Basic bookkeeping principles

- **Soft Skills:**
  - Attention to detail
  - Analytical thinking
  - Time management
  - Communication (explaining financial data to non-financial stakeholders)
  - Problem-solving
  - Honesty and integrity

## A: Actuary

### What is an Actuary?
An actuary is a financial professional who uses mathematics, statistics, and financial theories to assess and manage financial risks. They work primarily in insurance, pensions, and finance industries to calculate probabilities and costs of future events.

### What Do They Do in a Company?
- **Risk Assessment:** Analyze and quantify financial risks
- **Modeling:** Create mathematical models to predict future costs
- **Pricing:** Develop insurance premium pricing strategies
- **Valuation:** Value insurance liabilities and pension obligations
- **Stress Testing:** Test financial scenarios and market impacts
- **Reporting:** Present actuarial findings to management and regulators
- **Research:** Conduct research on mortality, morbidity, and market trends
- **Compliance:** Ensure compliance with regulatory requirements
- **Strategy:** Develop financial and risk management strategies
- **Consultation:** Provide expert advice on risk management

### Skills Required
- **Technical Skills:**
  - Advanced mathematics and statistics
  - Probability theory
  - Financial modeling and analysis
  - Actuarial software (Prophet, Embark, Excel)
  - Programming languages (Python, R, SQL)
  - Financial accounting
  - Regression analysis and data science
  - Strong Excel skills

- **Soft Skills:**
  - Analytical and critical thinking
  - Communication and presentation
  - Problem-solving
  - Attention to detail
  - Teamwork and collaboration
  - Business acumen

## A: Advocate

An advocate is a legal specialist in South Africa who provides legal representation in court. Advocates typically work independently or in chambers and are usually instructed by attorneys on behalf of clients. They specialize in litigation, criminal law, and specialized legal fields.
